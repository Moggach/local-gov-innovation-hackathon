**Problem Statement**

We aim to prevent homelessness by developing a tool that helps local
authorities identify the factors that increase a householdâ€™s likelihood
of becoming homeless or being at risk.

By securely combining and analysing multiple data sources to detect
early trajectories toward homelessness, the tool will enable timely,
targeted support before households reach crisis point.

This approach seeks to reduce the impact of homelessness on individuals
and families while helping local authorities manage system-wide demand
and alleviate the financial pressures associated with temporary
accommodation.

**Key Causes and Contributing Factors**

-   Substance misuse

-   Mental health challenges

-   Domestic abuse

-   Relationship breakdowns

-   Family disputes

-   Unaffordable rent

-   Poor condition of current property

-   Overcrowding

**Where People Go for Help (and Potential Data Touchpoints)**

These touchpoints indicate where early signals may exist and where data
might be accessed or aggregated (subject to governance and
availability):

**Financial / Housing Indicators**

-   Housing rents (arrears, payment issues)

-   Council Tax (CT) arrears

-   Housing Benefit / DWP interactions

-   Housing associations

-   Landlords register (upcoming legislation)

**Crisis Support & Advice**

-   Food banks

-   Day centres

-   Citizens Advice

-   Domestic abuse services

-   Community mental health teams

**Public Services & Community Indicators**

-   Schools (attendance, welfare concerns)

-   GP practices / health services

-   A&E admissions

-   Missed medical appointments

-   Crime data

-   Anti-social behaviour reports

**Digital Access & Engagement**

-   Library use (free Wi-Fi, study space)

-   Households with or without internet access

-   Employer referrals to support

-   Google Analytics of relevant council webpages (to monitor early
    demand signals)
